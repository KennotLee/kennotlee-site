Took this class over CS2040DE because I wanted to take it with my friends tbh. Also I did not really entrust a CS module to be handled by ECE department since I have heard feedback that the admin side of the module was not well run. I also wanted to challenge myself and just not coop myself up in Engineering. This module is probably harder than the DE variant. DE variant is filled with ISE Majors and Engineering Majors with Computing (Design and Engineering) Minor / 2nd Major while this was filled with DSA and BZA students. When comparing both variants I preferred the style done by SOC since it's more structured and the questions were reasonably challenging. (Or maybe I'm biased by the choice I have made)

Assignments

Labs and Tutorials

Both labs and tutorial are graded for attendance. Participation in tutorial just means you asked or answer questions as they go through the tutorial for the week. Labs are pseudocode that you submit to the TA and then which makes up the majority of the marks in the lab while finishing and submitting the java code is where you get the full marks. (Some degree of error is permitted so long as it is reasonable). This part was confusing initially since I was not used to writing pseudocode. They are not difficult since they go through the concepts they were just taught the previous week

Take Home Assignments

Take home assignments are more challenging than the average labs and around the same level as tutorials or harder. You just have to submit the code on Kattis and they may or may not have restrictions(I guess to force you to not smurf / use AI). The take home assignments are usually 1-2 weeks long and you can submit multiple times.

Visualgo Quiz

I have many complaints. However I will keep my rant short. Some of the questions are literally just counting lines and some are tracing how a hashmap works and everything in between in terms of how tedious. You miscount / off by one and boom 1 mark gone. This quiz may have costed me a whole grade range. It is also somewhat random since you can get an easy set of question or questions that just waste years of your life. You are allowed any online materials but no internet access.

Written Assessment

This is like your mini midterm I guess just to test your understanding of the concepts before you go off for recess week. All hardcopy materials and calculators allowed just like the midterms.

Midterm

This is where the questions get somewhat difficult and the bellcurve tanks significantly. The questions are mostly coming out with pseudocode and so long as you match ~95% of the logic you get most if not all the marks. The median consistently lands around 50% of the paper. Hardcopy materials and calculators allowed.

Final Exam

The finals test the later half of the course for the most part and requires concepts and additional layer of thought to solve the questions. So you can't copy paste the algorithms and have to understand the steps and how to appropriately modify the question to understand. Just like midterms hardcopy materials and calculators allowed.

Grades

Can't exactly remember but final grade was around 80 ±1 I got at or above Upper Quartile for all the assessments except for the VisuAlgo Quiz which I got 12 /15 and 10/15 or something (Don't be like me :P) Expected Grade: B/B+ (Based off of CS2030 and 3 days before final exam being told in a consult that I was below passing mark by my tutorial GA ;-;) Actual Grade: A

Part One: Assembly and C Programming

I have quite a lot to say about this class to be honest.

I thought this module would be light since there is the variant of CG2028 which is 2MC but I was dead wrong. The 2MC should be a 3MC this should be 6-7MC (I'm sorry... 8MC).

In short this can be split into two: First half teaching the fundamentals of assembly on the STM32 and the C language and the second half teaching how to communicate with all the devices on the STM32.

I don't think Assembly portion is not as heavy as although it's new once you understand bits and pointers it's not too difficult. Think of coding in Python / Java dividing into two. The lecturer does spend an excessive amount of time trying to explain pretty simple concepts. My mistake here was not learning it earlier on and cramming it within 2-3 hours.

C part was brutal to be honest. Most students would have experience python coming out of CS1010E. I was lucky that I knew Java (more like forced to ahem) so things like syntax was not an issue though the pointers operations were mostly new. The contents of C aren't too difficult but the fact that "Introduction to C" and "Advanced C Programming" is jsut 2 weeks is quite brutal.

I did not like how Prof Henry Tan went to poll out to the cohort for online lectures on zoom or f2f lectures. I would have opted for f2f since I am in the space to actually learn and actually focus. Once it moved online I stopped watching halfway into the first live lecture. He did crash out over low attendance (~40/2-300 or so) and moved one lecture to f2f (For context there are 2 lectures 2 hours at LT6 and a 1 hour at some UTown LT). I tend to not watch lectures live because it is not my style and watch recordings at a higher speed.

Part Two: Interfacing

This part was much more brutal than the first. You are given to watch lecture videos that are super daunting 4 sets of videos ranging from approx 15 to 40+ minutes. They are extremely content dense and sometimes it's still difficult to understand what is going on behind the scenes. A bunch of jargon is thrown without much explanation and you're expected to understand it. So cumulatively it took me 2x the duration to partially grasp the content.

Lectures were physical and not recorded and they weren't particularly helpful since he would go through the questions tested on finals that test on the week's topics.

Tutorials

Typical of Tutorials in NUS. They give out tutorials as a set of questions and then the tutor will go through. Attendance is not taken, it is taught by GA's

Labs

They happen every other week some weeks will not have some will have you just tinker with the STM32 and attendance is taken for the most part. The labs are also pairwork

Assignment / Project

There are two assignments that you do with your lab pair. For the first assignment, you are tasked to write a program in assembly that have some degree of complexity. While very simple in high level languages like C++ or Java.

The second assignment is a project where you are tasked to write a program in C that interfaces with the STM32 and some of its peripherals. It is a bit more complex than the first assignment and requires a lot of time to complete. You will have to demonstrate the project and it's enhancements (Like the bonus features to the lab GA). They will also test you on how much you understand the topics and you are only given the code and the data sheets no notes or anything. You will also have to prepare a set of presentation slides to present your project and the enhancements you have made.

Quizzes

They are done during lectures via Canvas, they will track attendance and it's held face to face. Some questions are fill in the blank while some are MCQs they do not test anything to advanced

Finals

The finals were the most brutal part and it is on Examplify with softcopy notes allowed. You will not have enough time to finish the paper as there are two coding questions done on examplify (I think it is eyeballed for correctness so if you code a basic loop you get a decent chunk of marks). The rest are MCQs, Short answer questions. They do not release PYPs for you ot try out so you don't really know how the true pacing of the exam is going to be like. So when I looked at the C part I was a bit overwhelmed by the amount of text on my Laptop screen. I have also found out that you cannot copy paste your answers from an IDE or anything so that drained a lot of my time since my implementation was for speed of finishing the question by using copy paste not really a short piece of code.

In the end I did leave about 30% of the paper blank and just hoped for the best. I did not have high expectations for this module to be honest since I most of the time I was already drained out from other classes.

Grades

  • Expected: B/B+
  • Actual: A-

I was not expecting it. My CAs were around Median for the most part I guess finals hard carried since I was a good 6% of overall grade behind my friend but still got the same grade range in the end.
Moral of the story: If you're cooked everyone is cooked too.

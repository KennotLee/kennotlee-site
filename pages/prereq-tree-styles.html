2. NUSMods-style dependency graph (brace connectors, colored nodes)

Recreated from a screenshot, now following the site's own line colors (--line-4 red for reverse dependents, --line-2 green for prerequisites, --line-3 orange for the course itself — swap city theme in the footer and these recolor too) instead of fixed hex. Connected by plain right-angle brace connectors, relationship spelled out directly on the line ("needs" / "all of" / "one of"). Shows BOTH directions at once: what EE3731C needs (right side) and what needs EE3731C back (left side).

Boxed nodes are real links here (href="#" placeholders — wire real hrefs once this is real data) and lift on hover; the three plain-text nodes (EE4604, EE4605, EE2012A) have no page to link to, so they're inert <div>s with no hover — same "hover only if there's a link" rule as the rest of the site. 3. NUSMods dependency graph, large example (5 "one of" groups + a nested "at least 2 of")

Recreated from a second screenshot: CS4243 is the course being viewed (orange, "current"), CS6240 is a single reverse dependent that needs it ("needs", red), and CS4243 itself needs "all of" 5 breadth requirement groups (each "one of", teal) — 35 leaf modules total. MA1507 (bottom-right, plain text — no page of its own) has its own further requirement one level deeper: "at least 2 of" MA1512/MA1511. Every coordinate here (all ~50 stub lines, 5 local brackets, the shared "all of" spine, and the nested branch) came out of one small arithmetic pass over a fixed row height, so line endpoints and box positions can't drift out of sync with each other the way hand-eyeballed numbers can — see the comment on ".gallery-diagram" in stylesheets/style.css for the OTHER half of that (matching this div's inline width/height to the viewBox below). GALLERY-ONLY markup.
